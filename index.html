Why Beeswax and Beekeeping?

Cling films and plastic bags used to protect food in kitchens create serious environmental pollution due to their non-recyclable nature. In local markets, plastic derivatives are used unconsciously and preventive waste measures were not available.

In line with this observation, to eliminate general knowledge deficiencies and highlight the importance of bees for the ecosystem, we are launching this project.

12 Month Process

Project Roadmap

Sustainable transformation through preparation, implementation, evaluation and dissemination phases.

Month 1 Preparation

Kick-off Meeting & Planning

Sharing the project vision with all stakeholders and initiating the process.

  • Kick-off meeting with stakeholders
  • Establishing communication channels
  • Nature walks and social bonding events
  • Task distribution and partner research
Month 2 Preparation

Digital Infrastructure & Supply

Creating digital presence by setting up social media accounts and the project website.

  • Launching Instagram, YouTube, TikTok accounts
  • Content scripts and challenge topic selection
  • Website setup and educational content upload
  • Connecting with university communities
Month 3 Preparation

Partnerships, Supply & Cost Analysis

Partnership with local beekeepers and supply planning for workshop materials.

  • Partnership meetings with local beekeepers
  • Workshop material info and supply partnerships
  • Finalizing facility and factory tour plans
  • Cost analysis: cotton fabric, resin, natural oils
IMPLEMENTATION PHASE START
Month 4 Implementation

Field Promotion & Participant Selection

Reaching the target audience through promotional stalls at the university and local locations.

  • Promotional stalls at Gaziantep University
  • Plastic harm awareness and sustainable alternatives
  • Registration of 100-person target group (ages 18-30)
Month 5 Implementation

Beekeeping Seminar

Discovering the critical importance of bees to the ecosystem with a professional beekeeper.

  • Importance of bees for ecosystem and biodiversity
  • Theoretical seminar by professional beekeeper
  • Interactive activity and seminar analysis
Month 6 Implementation

Visual Learning & Facility Tours

Conveying the 'prevention before waste' vision through recycling facility and factory tours.

  • Recycling facility tour with field staff presentation
  • Waste conversion forms and plastic recycling challenges
  • Photo/video content creation during tours
  • Beekeeping products factory tours
2 Workshops
Month 7 Implementation

Beeswax & Beeswax Wrap Workshops

Plastic-free kitchen goal through extracting beeswax from waste combs and producing beeswax wraps.

  • Workshop: extracting beeswax from waste combs
  • Wrap production: baking, ironing, or dipping techniques
  • Beeswax wrap benefits and usage guidelines
  • "Plastic-Free Kitchen" challenge launch
Month 8 Implementation

Propolis Extraction Workshop

Hands-on teaching of propolis extraction techniques using olive oil and alcohol.

  • Extraction practice with olive oil and alcohol
  • Expert briefing on propolis benefits
  • Observational participant experience with beekeeper
Month 9 Implementation

Bee Venom & Royal Jelly Workshop

Discovering royal jelly harvesting and the health/cosmetic potential of bee venom.

  • Royal jelly harvesting from queen cells
  • Demo of collection with vacuum devices
  • Health and cosmetic benefits briefing
  • Economic value creation potential presentation
EVALUATION PHASE
Month 10 Evaluation

Monitoring & Data Collection

Data-driven analysis of the 40% plastic reduction target using Agile management approach.

  • Pre vs. post comparison of plastic bag/cling film usage
  • Website access and social media engagement rates
  • Impact measurement via challenge feedback
  • Reporting dissemination data
Month 11 Evaluation

Evaluation & Process Improvement

Comprehensive evaluation of all activities in terms of effectiveness, efficiency and sustainability.

  • Assessment tests and evaluation forms analysis
  • Individual feedback and behavior change analysis
  • Process optimization via Agile feedback loops
  • Written internal report and accountability document
DISSEMINATION & CLOSING
Month 12 Dissemination

Dissemination, Certification & Closing

Sharing project outcomes, publishing digital toolkit and Youthpass certification.

  • Creating comprehensive project report
  • Publishing Digital Toolkit as open access
  • Organizing closing and dissemination seminar
  • Youthpass certificates for individual development
  • Sharing best practices and participant experiences
